Pet Insurance Questions from Houston Pet Owners

How much does pet insurance cost in Houston?

Pet insurance in Houston typically costs $30–$70 per month for dogs and $15–$40 per month for cats depending on your pet's age, breed, and the coverage level you choose. Texas has a competitive pet insurance market — using a comparison tool like PetInsurer.com to see multiple quotes at once is the best way to find the right plan at the right price.

Is heartworm prevention covered by pet insurance in Texas?

Heartworm prevention itself is generally not covered by standard accident and illness policies — it falls under preventive or wellness care. However many providers offer optional wellness add-ons that include heartworm prevention. In Houston's climate year-round heartworm prevention is essentially mandatory, so a wellness add-on can be cost-effective. Heartworm treatment if your pet contracts the disease is generally covered under accident and illness policies if not pre-existing.

Which insurance is best for large breed dogs common in Texas?

For large breed dogs in Houston — Labs, Goldens, German Shepherds, and similar breeds — Trupanion is a top pick because they have no payout caps which matters for the joint and hip issues large breeds commonly develop. Fetch is also strong for mixed-breed rescues from BARC and Houston SPCA. Compare both on PetInsurer.com to find the right fit.

When should I enroll my new BARC rescue in insurance?

Enroll as soon as possible after adoption — ideally within 14 days. Most pet insurance policies have a waiting period of 14–30 days before coverage begins, and conditions that develop or are diagnosed before enrollment are excluded as pre-existing. BARC adopters are commonly bringing home an adult dog whose history is partially unknown, which makes early enrollment especially important.
