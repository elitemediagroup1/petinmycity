🚨 Emergency vet guidance

When to seek emergency care

If your pet shows any of the following, go to an emergency vet or 24-hour animal hospital immediately:

  • Difficulty breathing or choking
  • Severe bleeding or trauma
  • Suspected poisoning or toxin ingestion
  • Repeated vomiting or diarrhea (especially with blood)
  • Inability to urinate (especially male cats)
  • Collapse, seizures, or unconsciousness
  • Bloated/distended abdomen (especially large dogs)
  • Heatstroke symptoms (excessive panting, weakness)

Quick answers

How do I find a good vet?

Use the ZIP search above to see Google Maps results — check ratings, hours, and reviews. Call ahead to confirm they accept new patients.

How much does a vet visit cost?

A wellness exam is $50–$150. Vaccines run $15–$30 each. Emergency visits start around $150 and can climb fast without insurance.

When should my pet first see a vet?

Within the first week after adoption or purchase — even if records came with the pet. Puppies and kittens need their first checkup by 8 weeks.
